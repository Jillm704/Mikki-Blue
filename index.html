What I Do

Being active is the most important thing in my life. Playing for leisure is fun, but my real passion is competing. I train at a bare minimum 25 hours per week, depending on my owner or trainer's availability, for all kinds of competitions. I have attended numerous dog shows in the southern region to show off my abilities in tricky obstacle courses, and I have always placed in the top ten — even the top five on several occasions.

How I train

If there's a big space, whether indoors or outdoors, then I can train. Typically I can be found training indoors, in a similar environment to that of a professional competition. This requires my owner and I to rent out space at our local gym — and sometimes I get a discount for being adorable. Regardless, I can train outside just as well as I can train inside. My mom owns a gargantuan yard, where she sets up all kinds of obstacle courses and practice runs for me.
